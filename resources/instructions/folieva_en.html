INSTRUCTION

for medical use of the drug



FOLIC ACID

( FOLIC ACID )


Composition :

active substance : folic acid;

1 tablet contains folic acid 1 mg;

Excipients: glucose monohydrate, stearic acid.



Dosage form. Tablets.

Main physical and chemical properties: round tablets with a flat surface and chamfer, light yellow. Heterogeneity of color and insignificant impregnations are allowed.



Pharmacotherapeutic group. Antianemic drugs. Folic acid and its derivatives.

ATX code B03B B01.



Pharmacological properties.

Pharmacodynamics.

Folic acid belongs to the B vitamins and is synthesized in the human body by intestinal microflora. After taking the drug, folic acid is reduced to tetrahydrofolic acid, which is a coenzyme involved in various metabolic processes. It is necessary for the normal development of other blood cells, including the formation and maturation of megaloblasts and the formation of normoblasts. In combination with vitamin B 12 (cyanocobalamin) stimulates hematopoiesis, in part - erythropoiesis. Participates in the synthesis of amino acids (including methionine, serine, glycine and histidine), nucleic acids, purines, pyrimidines, participates in choline metabolism.

During pregnancy, folic acid protects the body from teratogenic factors.

Pharmacokinetics.

After oral administration, folic acid is well and completely absorbed from the digestive tract, mainly in the upper duodenum and proximal small intestine. Almost completely binds to blood plasma proteins. About 87% of folate in the blood is in erythrocytes, 10-13% - in serum. From the blood, folates enter the liver, where they are activated by the enzyme dihydrofolate reductase, converted into tetrahydrofolic acid. The maximum concentration in the blood is reached in 30-60 minutes. Excreted by the kidneys mainly as metabolites; if the dose significantly exceeds the daily requirement of folic acid, the kidneys begin to secrete the vitamin in an unchanged state. Excreted in the urine by glomerular filtration; 5 mg of orally taken folic acid is excreted after 5 hours. During hemodialysis it is removed from the circulatory system.

 

 

Clinical characteristics.

Indications .

Macrocytic anemias, in particular megaloblastic anemias in pregnant women; for the treatment of sprue in order to normalize hematopoiesis, eliminate or reduce the clinical manifestations of the disease. Anemia and leukopenia caused by ionizing radiation, chemicals, including drugs; anemia caused by resection of the stomach or part of the intestine, megaloblastic anemia caused by intestinal tuberculosis and chronic gastroenteritis (in these diseases is shown without the development of anemia), pernicious anemia, pellagra. As an aid in depression and anxiety syndrome; in the treatment of cervical dysplasia.

 

Contraindications .

Hypersensitivity to folic acid or to components of the drug, vitamin B 12 deficiency , malignant neoplasms, malignant anemia, untreated cobalamin deficiency.

 

Interaction with other medicinal products and other forms of interaction .

In patients with folate deficiency, the use of folic acid may reduce the levels of phenobarbital, phenytoin and primidone in the blood plasma and cause an epileptic seizure.

Oral contraceptives, ethanol, sulfasalazine, cycloserine, glutethimide and methotrexate may affect folate metabolism.

Acetylsalicylic acid may increase the elimination of folic acid. Folate increases the efficiency of lithium. Nitric oxide can cause acute folic acid deficiency.

Folic acid deficiency can be caused by drugs such as anti-TB drugs, folic acid antagonists such as pyrimethamine, triamterene, trimethoprim.

Assimilation of folic acid is reduced by concomitant use with analgesics, anticonvulsants, antacids, chloramphenicol, neomycin, polymyxins, antibiotics, sulfonamides, cytostatics.

Folic acid may reduce the absorption of zinc in the intestine.

Do not use together with mineral acids, alkalis, reducing agents, as inactivation of folic acid.

Avoid simultaneous combination with fluorouracil. Antacids containing aluminum or magnesium may reduce folic acid uptake, so patients should be advised to take antacids 2 hours after folic acid administration.

Folic acid increases phenytoin metabolism. Cases of decreased or altered absorption may occur with the simultaneous use of cholestyramine and folic acid. Therefore, the drug should be taken 1 hour before or 4-6 hours after taking cholestyramine.

 

Features of application.

The drug should be used with caution in patients with anemia of unknown etiology, as folic acid may interfere with the diagnosis of malignant anemia by improving the hematological manifestations of the disease, while allowing the progression of neurological complications.

This medicine should not be used in healthy pregnant women, for whom the lowest doses may be recommended, but should be prescribed to pregnant women with folic acid deficiency or to women at risk of recurrence of neural tube defects.

Prolonged intake of folic acid (especially in high doses) is not recommended due to the possible decrease in the concentration of cyanocobalamin (vitamin B 12 ) in the blood.

In the treatment of folic acid requires systematic monitoring of blood.

Elderly people should be tested for cobalamin absorption before starting long-term therapy.

The drug contains glucose, which should be considered in patients with diabetes mellitus and patients with glucose-galactose malabsorption syndrome.

Folic acid should be used in combination with other drugs: in pernicious anemia - only in combination with cyanocobalamin, because folic acid, stimulating hematopoiesis, does not prevent the development of neurological complications (eg, funicular myelosis); in sprue - in combination with ascorbic acid, cyanocobalamin, with chemotherapy.

 

Use during pregnancy or breastfeeding.

Folic acid deficiency, or folic acid metabolism disorders, is associated with birth defects and some neural tube defects. Interference with folic acid metabolism or folic acid deficiency due to the action of certain drugs, such as anticonvulsants, anticancer drugs, in early pregnancy, leads to congenital anomalies. Lack of vitamin or its metabolites can also affect miscarriage and intrauterine growth retardation.

For therapeutic purposes, the drug can be used as prescribed by a doctor, in the doses and duration specified in the instructions.

 

Ability to influence the speed of reaction when driving a car or other machinery .

There are no data on the negative effects of folic acid on the ability to drive vehicles.

 

Method of application and dosage.

Take folic acid orally after meals.

Dosage and duration of treatment is determined by the doctor depending on the nature and course of the disease.

For therapeutic purposes, the drug is prescribed to adults 1-2 mg (1-2 tablets) 1-3 times a day. The maximum daily dose is 5 mg (5 tablets).

Children over 3 years of age, depending on the age of the child and the nature of the disease, the drug should be prescribed 1 mg (1 tablet) 1-2 times a day. The maximum daily dose is 2 mg (2 tablets).

The usual course of treatment is 20-30 days.

In macrocytic anemia, the drug should be prescribed to adults at 5 mg 2-3 times a day for 10-15 days.

Pregnant women with low hemoglobin in the first trimester of pregnancy should be given folic acid at a dose of 4 mg per day in combination with iron supplements for 3 months. Then reduce the dose of folic acid to 4 mg once a week.

 

Children.

Contraindicated in children under 3 years.

 

Overdose .

Symptoms: Folic acid overdose may mask vitamin B12 deficiency .

Prolonged and significant excess of the recommended doses can cause dangerous accumulation of folacin crystals, which leads to intoxication of the body and increased side effects.

Treatment : drug withdrawal, symptomatic therapy.

 

Adverse reactions .

The drug is well tolerated.

- from the digestive tract: rarely - nausea, vomiting, bloating, flatulence, bitterness, very rarely - anorexia;

- from the immune system : rarely - allergic reactions, including skin rash, erythema, urticaria, itching and respiratory disorders due to bronchospasm, hypotension, anaphylactic reactions;

- from the central nervous system: fever, sleep disturbance, convulsions;

- from the urinary system: hypertrophy of epithelial cells in the tubules of the kidneys and dysfunction.
